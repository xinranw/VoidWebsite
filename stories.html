Eat our dinner, check into the hotel, and Lucky convinces me to call my house to have someone check my email to see if the tourney's still on. It is of course cancelled, or else this wouldn't be a good story. So people resort to the 40's and caucasians and our little green friend and we decide to party it up at the richmond campus. Haverford Layout Pigout Spring 2000 (sort of)

After coming back from Haverford the Saturday of Fling, it was about 5 o'clock, so after taking a shower, I immediately started drinking to make up for lost time. Every one else around me was gone at this point since they started when they got up. By 7 o'clock I had caught up to everyone, and by 8, I think I surpassed them. We were all getting hungry so we went to this festival in the middle of the high rises that supposedly had food. It turned out that all they had were pretzels. In my disgust (and drunken state) I took about 20 leis that they were handing out, and ran away trying to put them on people. That was the last thing I remember until 10 o'clock at night. In the meantime, we evidentially went to a Cav's, I ordered a chicken parm sandwich, got up to go to the bathroom, and never came back. My friends were freaking out thinking that I had passed out somewhere so they searched the whole restaurant including the ladies room, and the surrounding block. I was nowhere to be found. They ended up paying for my meal, and putting it in a doggie bag. They went back to our hall and started asking people if they had seen me. They had not. I guess they eventually gave me up for lost.

In the meanwhile, at around 10, I "awoke" from my memory blackout, and found myself in a part of Philly that I had never seen before. I started freaking out, so my "logical mind" somehow told me to get on a highway since that would be the fastest or easiest way back. In my mind, I thought I was following someone as I ran along the shoulder of a major superhighway for a good 15 minutes, including over a bridge. I either eventually realized what I was doing was stupid or thought that I was going to stay on the highway for too long, so I crossed it, and attempted to run up a steep embankment. I obviously fell back down. I repeated this about 3 more times before finally making it to the top into a wooded area of some sort. All the while, including on the highway, I was sprinting since I really, really wanted to get back as fast as I could so I obviously fell down a lot which resulting in torn pants, cut up hands and arms, and me being generally soaked since it rained all day. I ran in the woods for a while alongside a fence with barbwire on top, and on the other side were train tracks after a 15-foot drop-off. The tracks eventually came to the same level as the forest, so I took off my shirt, threw it over the barbed wire, climbed the fence, and fell onto the other side. After crossing the tracks, I somehow made my way to a corporate park. (at the time i thought thats what it was.  it was probably just part of fairmount).

After running around there for a while, I made it to city streets. This should have been fine then, but I was only a freshman, and still very naiive and drunk. I asked a guy sort of passed on a bench how to get back to 37th and Spruce.  He gave me directions, so i started running.  He then started shouting that i was going the wrong way, but he was a scary homeless man so i kept on running.  I eventually found my way to the art museum. I had run from campus to there a couple times so I thought I was in the clear. I was wrong. It was not the Art museum.  I guess it  was one of those mansions in Fairmout Park. It took another 45 minutes of sprinting this way and that before I finally made my way back to campus. It was around 12:30 or 1 at this point. I promptly took off my ruined clothes, washed the blood off my hands, set my alarm and went to bed. The next morning at 7:45 I woke up for ultimate with incredibly sore muscles, and my hands wouldn't work since they were so bruised and battered. When I got back to campus that afternoon, everyone was asking, what happened to you? Where did you go? And they had as good an idea as I did.
